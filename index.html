FunPassGen

by Alex Diwa and Jade Rosario

FunPassGen - or Fun Password Generator - is a terminal application written in Ruby. FunPassGen generates a secure password based on the user's responses to prompts, as well as a memorable story that enables easy password recall. Additionally, the app can encrypt the password for the user.

Alex Diwa

I’m a developer in training at Coder Academy. Before entering tech, I was a science research student who studied the structure and function of materials and proteins. I’m passionate mental health and social justice, and enjoy yoga, bushwalking and being around trees.

I have always loved problem solving. I took a liking to coding when I was an awkward 12-year-old, when learning basic HTML and CSS allowed me to express myself creatively and connect with others in ways that I found difficult to do at school.

In more recent years, working in science and science research, I have experienced first-hand the capabilities of software and web platforms in driving new discoveries and innovations in research and in education.

I am particularly inspired by the potential that technology has in transforming the lives of individuals and communities. This includes addressing social issues and inequities, empowering people from underrepresented and/or marginalised groups, and democratising access to resources and education.

Ultimately, this is what drives me to learn how to code, each and every day, and I am eager to work on projects that have a social impact and that can improve people’s lives.
